Integrator flagship

TACTIME

Live

Customers want a cycle time on every proposal. Estimating it properly means modelling the cell — robot, EOAT, pick/place poses, dwell times — and most shops don't have hours to spend on something that may not win. So they eyeball it, pad it, and hope. Sometimes that's the difference between winning the job and bleeding on it.

TACTIME flips that. Upload the customer's DXF, set a handful of cell parameters, and the module returns a realistic cycle-time estimate in seconds — not in someone's head. Export a branded PDF report straight to the customer, or link the estimate to a JOBS quote so the cell cost and the cycle time live on the same record.

Turns guess-and-pad into a number you can actually put on a quote.

Features

DXF upload, parametric cell modelling, cycle-time estimation, branded PDF reports, JOBS quote linking, revision history per estimate

Replaces

Cycle-time spreadsheets, whiteboard math, "I'll get back to you Monday"

Saves

Hours per quote; lets you respond same-day with a defensible number

JOBS

Live

Clients, cells, quotes, purchase orders, invoices, expenses, and profitability — in most integration shops, that's a CRM, a quoting tool, a spreadsheet, and the bookkeeper's QuickBooks file. JOBS pulls it into one place, organised around the unit of work that matters: the cell.

Every cell has its own record. Quotes are built inline with an embedded line-item builder and exported as branded PDFs. POs to your robot and EOAT suppliers attach directly to the cell they belong to. Invoices and payments roll up. Labour hours (from TIMESHEET) flow in automatically. At any point you can see the live margin on the cell — not just the top-line revenue.

Replaces four tools with one, and answers the question every shop owner cares about: are we making money on this cell?

Features

Client records, per-cell project tracking, embedded quote builder with PDF preview, purchase orders, invoices & payments, expense capture, labour roll-up from TIMESHEET, live per-cell profit

Replaces

Generic CRM + quoting tool + project tracker + expense spreadsheet

Saves

Hours per week on admin; real visibility into per-cell margin

STOCK

Live

Robot parts, sensors, EOAT components, pneumatics, electrical, fasteners — tracked in an Excel spreadsheet, with a piece of paper taped to the stockroom door as the "system." No matter how many reminders went out, nobody picked up the pen. Items went missing. Stock ran out without warning. Cells got held up waiting on materials that should have been on the shelf.

Instead of locking the shop into expensive inventory software, we built a stock tracking module right into the HUB. Imported the old spreadsheet directly. Integrated a cheap barcode scanner. Scan in, scan out — pop-up confirms the transaction. Everyone uses it because it's faster than not using it.

Then we added an AI layer on top. When a part drops below its reorder threshold, the system drafts a replenishment email to the supplier requesting a quote. When the quote comes back, AI parses it, logs the pricing, and flags it for approval. One click confirms the order. No one has to check stock levels or remember to reorder.

A spreadsheet and a piece of paper turned into a self-managing parts crib.

Features

Barcode scanner integration, bin-level tracking, part search, reorder thresholds, low-stock alerts, spreadsheet import, AI-drafted supplier replenishment, quote parsing and approval workflow

Built in

3 days (+ AI replenishment layer added after)

Replaces

Spreadsheets, paper logs, Fishbowl, inFlow, Sortly, Zoho Inventory

Saves

Hours of manual counting; eliminates the cells held up waiting on parts that "should have been here"

SOP

Live

Every cell ships with documentation — install steps, commissioning checklists, operator instructions, maintenance procedures. Most shops pay over $10K/year for documentation software that uses AI to generate written work instructions from video. The tools work — video in, step-by-step instructions out, clean documents in minutes. But at that price, it's hard to justify for the handful of techs who actually use it.

So we built the same thing, better — custom to the shop's workflow, tied into their existing systems, branded for the customer the cell is shipping to. Beautiful documents in minutes. Same output, fraction of the cost, no annual renewal fees.

One person. Less than a week. Now the shop owns the tool outright.

Built in

Less than 1 week

Replaces

Dozuki, SwipeGuide, Poka, and similar documentation platforms

Saves

~$10,000+/year in subscription costs

CAL

Live

The shop owner needed a long-term planning calendar — which tech is on which cell, when installs are happening, where the service crew needs to be next week and next month. Not meetings. Not hourly scheduling. Just a clear, visual layout of who's doing what and when across builds, installs, and commissioning.

Every calendar app on the market is designed for meetings, not work assignments. So he went back to an Excel sheet. But nobody knew when it changed, and updates got lost.

We built CAL in a single day. A clean, visual long-term planning calendar inside the HUB, colour-coded by cell. When anything changes, every employee gets notified automatically. And if a tech still wants Outlook, they can sync it — both calendars stay in step.

Built in a day. Solved a problem the owner had been fighting for years.

Features

Long-term project calendar, drag-and-drop scheduling, automatic change notifications, Outlook sync, team-wide visibility, colour-coded per cell

Built in

1 day

Replaces

Excel scheduling sheets, whiteboards, generic calendar apps

Saves

Eliminates confusion over schedule changes; the whole shop sees the same plan

TIMESHEET

Live

No separate payroll subscription. No CSV exports. Hours in, profit updated.

Features

Replaces

Saves

Eliminates duplicate entry between time tracking and per-cell costing
