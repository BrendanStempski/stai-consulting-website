Stock Keeping

Live

Parts were tracked in an Excel spreadsheet. A piece of paper taped to the stockroom door was the "system" — log items in and out with a pen. No matter how many reminders went out, nobody picked up the pen. Items went missing. Stock ran out without warning. Jobs got held up waiting on materials that should have been on the shelf.

Instead of locking into expensive inventory software, we built a simple stock keeping app right into their HUB. Imported their old spreadsheet directly. Bought a cheap barcode scanner and integrated it into the app. Now instead of picking up a pen, they pull a trigger — scan the item in, scan it out. A pop-up confirms the transaction. Everyone actually uses it because it's faster than not using it.

One person. A few days. Problem solved.

Then we took it a step further. Once the stock data was digital and accurate, we built an AI agent on top of it. When an item drops below its reorder threshold, the system automatically drafts a replenishment email to the supplier requesting a quote. When the quote comes back, the AI parses the response, logs the pricing, and flags it for approval. One click to confirm the order. No one has to check stock levels. No one has to remember to reorder. No one has to write an email. The system watches the shelves and handles it.

What started as a spreadsheet and a piece of paper is now a self-managing inventory system.

Features

Barcode scanner integration, bin-level tracking, item search, quantity logging, reorder threshold alerts, low-stock dashboard notifications, spreadsheet import, AI-powered auto-replenishment emails to suppliers, quote parsing and approval workflow

Built in

3 days (+ AI replenishment layer added after)

Replaces

Spreadsheets, paper logs, Fishbowl, inFlow Inventory

Saves

Hours of manual counting, eliminates surprise stockouts and missing materials

TripleCAL

Live

The owner needed a simple long-term planning calendar — who's assigned to what job, which projects are running when, where people need to be next week and next month. Not meetings. Not hourly scheduling. Just a clear, visual layout of who's doing what and when.

He hated Outlook. Hated every calendar app he tried. They were all designed for meetings and appointments, not job assignments and project timelines. So he went back to an Excel sheet. But nobody knew when it changed. It lived on one computer. Updates got lost.

So we built TripleCAL in a single day. A clean, simple long-term planning calendar built into their HUB. When anything changes, every employee gets notified automatically. And if they still want Outlook, they can import TripleCAL straight into it — both calendars stay synced.

Simple tool. Built in a day. Solved a problem the owner had been fighting for years.

Features

Long-term job/project calendar, drag-and-drop scheduling, automatic change notifications to employees, Outlook calendar import/sync, team-wide visibility

Built in

1 day

Replaces

Excel scheduling sheets, Outlook (for this use case), whiteboards

Saves

Eliminates confusion over schedule changes, everyone sees the same plan
